Our Roots

The Swerve Racing Story

Swerve Racing was born from a shared passion for engineering and speed. We compete in STEM Racing India - a global motorsports challenge where students design, manufacture, and race miniature Formula 1 cars using professional CAD software. Our cars are powered by a single compressed CO2 cartridge and hit speeds over 80 km/hr in under a second.

Our members have already competed at the national level, earning the Research & Design award, reaching knockout runner-up at regionals, and earning a nomination for the prestigious Innovation Award at nationals. Every iteration, every late night in the lab, every simulation run has been building toward one goal: the global stage.

What is STEM Racing?

Students from around the world design, manufacture, and race miniature Formula 1 cars - but it's far more than just speed. Teams must manage a brand, secure sponsorships, build portfolios, and present at a Pit Display to judges, parents, and industry professionals. Regionals alone see up to 100 teams and 500+ participants.

Engineering

How We Built Our Car

  • deployed_code
    Rear Wing - Downforce

    Designed using a downward airfoil - the inverse of an airplane wing - to generate downforce that keeps the car pinned to the track at high speeds.

  • architecture
    Main Body - Drag Reduction

    Curved to minimise drag. Side pods divert air sideways and away from the rear wheels using the Coanda effect, proven through multiple CFD simulation runs.

  • science
    Wheels & Bearings - Mass Optimisation

    Wheels are hollow to reduce mass. Fixed axles carry bearings directly attached to the wheels, minimising rotational friction for maximum acceleration.
